What is ALS?

The Alternative Learning System (ALS) is a parallel learning system in the Philippines that provides a practical option for those who cannot access or finish formal education. It is designed to help out-of-school youth, adults, and other learners develop literacy skills, complete elementary and secondary education, and pursue lifelong learning opportunities.

Our Mission

To provide accessible, flexible, and relevant educational opportunities for all learners outside the formal school system, helping them achieve their full potential and become productive members of society.

Our Vision

A nation where every Filipino has access to quality education and lifelong learning—regardless of age, background, or circumstance—contributing to a more inclusive, educated, and empowered community.

What Alternative Learning System Does

Basic Literacy Programs

Develops fundamental reading, writing, and numeracy skills for those who need them.

Continuing Education

Allows learners to complete elementary and secondary levels through community-based sessions.

Flexible Learning

Uses modules, learning centers, and alternative delivery modes to adapt to learners' needs.

A&E Test Preparation

Prepares learners for the Accreditation and Equivalency Test for employment or higher education.

About Our Cluster

The Alternative Learning System (ALS) in Bukidnon Cluster I serves the municipalities of Manolo Fortich, Libona, Baungon, and Malitbog. Our mission is to provide out-of-school youth and adults with quality basic education through flexible learning programs.

Our team of dedicated educators and community facilitators work tirelessly to bring education closer to underserved communities, helping learners achieve their dreams and improve their quality of life.
